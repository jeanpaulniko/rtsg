## Key Principles (From Niko's Training History)

### 1. Two Halves of Strength
Strength = f(Muscle Cross-Section, Neural Recruitment)
- These are **independent sub-dimensions** (learned from Cirque du Soleil people)
- A small person with high neural recruitment beats a large person with low recruitment
- Training both simultaneously produces multiplicative (not additive) gains
- Same mathematics as cross-dimensional edges: C(2,2) = 1 edge, but the edge is multiplicative

### 2. CNS Irradiation
- Contracting muscles in one body part increases neural drive to all other parts
- Squeezing a fist with one hand measurably increases grip strength of the other
- Full-body tension (glutes, legs, core) during upper body exercises increases output
- This IS cross-dimensional activation within the Kinesthetic sub-space

### 3. Asymmetric Loading
- 100lb asymmetric dumbbell clean-and-press, one per side per day
- Dynamic instability forces proprioceptive sub-dimension to maximum activation
- The asymmetry creates a **perturbation** that the body must solve in real-time
- This is the physical equivalent of the hypervisor switching law: which stabilizer muscle takes control?

### 4. Cold Exposure as CNS Priming
- Cold exposure before training increases neural recruitment
- The cold activates sympathetic nervous system, increasing available neural drive
- Not about the cold itself — about what the cold does to the motor neuron pool

### 5. Minimum Effective Dose (Maintenance Protocol)
- One-arm pull-up: one rep per side per day maintains the capacity
- The Cirque du Soleil insight: maintenance requires far less volume than acquisition
- The body follows the same least-action principle as consciousness: minimum energy to maintain the structure

### 6. Thick Bar Training
- Incomplete grip forces greater forearm activation
- Greater forearm activation irradiates to entire upper body
- The "disadvantage" of the thick bar IS the training effect

### 7. Fingertip Pull-ups
- 50 reps on door ledges
- Extreme grip strength development
- Tendon conditioning (the limiting factor for one-arm pull-ups)
- The relationship between scar tissue and training limits
