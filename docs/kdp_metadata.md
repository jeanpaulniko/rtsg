## Book 1: RTSG Monograph

**Title:** Relational Three-Space Geometry: A Unified Framework

**Subtitle:** Intelligence, Consciousness, and Mathematics as Geometric Structure

**Author:** Jean-Paul Niko

**Description (4000 char max):**
Relational Three-Space Geometry (RTSG) presents a unified mathematical framework that bridges consciousness, intelligence, and physics through geometric structure. This monograph develops the complete theory from first principles: beginning with Ideometrics — a formal theory of ideas as geometric objects measurable against collective consciousness — through the Three Spaces architecture that decomposes reality into relational, topological, and geometric dimensions.

The framework introduces prime idea decomposition, where compound ideas factor into typed primes across eight intelligence dimensions, yielding canonical spectra for any cognitive object. Time-money utility theory projects cognitive utility onto real-world resource axes, enabling spectral producer-consumer matching. The work contains 68 definitions, 19 complete proofs, 11 worked examples, and 7 testable predictions.

Chapters cover consciousness ontology via fiber bundles and actualization operators, affective geometry formalizing emotional experience, emergence through filter formalism, temporal intelligence calculus, the IdeaRank algorithm for idea depth measurement, substrate independence theorems, and a universal taxonomy of cognitive objects. The final synthesis chapter unifies all threads into a single coherent geometric picture.

This is foundational work at the intersection of mathematical physics, cognitive science, and category theory — written for researchers, graduate students, and anyone seeking a rigorous geometric theory of mind.

## Book 2: RTSG Companion Papers

**Title:** Relational Three-Space Geometry: Companion Papers

**Subtitle:** Applications Across Disciplines

**Author:** Jean-Paul Niko

**Description (4000 char max):**
This collection presents 16 companion papers applying the Relational Three-Space Geometry (RTSG) framework — also known as Intelligence as Geometry (IAG) — across the full spectrum of human knowledge. Each paper takes the core mathematical machinery of RTSG and demonstrates its explanatory and predictive power within a specific discipline.

The papers span consciousness studies (including a standalone actualization architecture with fiber bundles and Born rule derivation), federated consciousness theory for cognitive assemblies, economics (spectral matching and utility decomposition), psychiatry (geometric models of mental illness), sociology (collective intelligence dynamics), machine learning (intelligence-native architectures), political science (power geometry), anthropology (cultural idea spectra), psychology (eight-dimensional intelligence profiles), neuroscience (neural substrate mappings), education (learning as geometric traversal), linguistics (Lojban as formal cognitive syntax), philosophy (from Plato's Forms to RTSG's Three Spaces), and nature taxonomy (consciousness across biological kingdoms).

Also included is the CogOS paper, which presents a complete software engineering architecture for cognitive operating systems built on RTSG principles.

Each paper is self-contained and can be read independently, though together they demonstrate the remarkable breadth of a single geometric framework applied to domains traditionally considered incommensurable. Written for researchers and advanced students across all disciplines represented.
