## The Science: Maximum Polyphonic Density

### The Question
How many simultaneous independent melodies can the human auditory system track?

### Known Limits
- **Conscious tracking**: 3-5 independent melodic streams (trained listeners up to 6-7)
- **Preconscious processing**: the auditory cortex processes FAR more than consciousness tracks — at least 8-12 independent streams are neurally encoded even when the listener cannot consciously separate them
- **Critical band theory**: the cochlea divides frequencies into ~24 critical bands (Bark scale). Each band can carry an independent melodic stream without masking
- **Practical maximum**: ~12-16 independent melodic streams across the full frequency range before psychoacoustic masking degrades separation

### The Inverse Fourier Insight

Any complex waveform can be decomposed into its frequency components (Fourier analysis). The reverse (Fourier synthesis) constructs a complex waveform FROM frequency components.

RTSG Audio applies inverse Fourier synthesis to **melodic streams**:
- Each frequency band carries an independent melody
- The melodies are mathematically constructed to maximize cross-stream interaction (harmonic, rhythmic, contrapuntal)
- The composite signal is a single audio stream that the ear receives as one sound but the auditory cortex decomposes into multiple threads
- The brain MUST process the polyphony even if conscious attention only tracks 3-5 streams

## Melodic Construction Algorithm

The melodies are not random. They are constructed using:

1. **Contrapuntal rules** — each pair of voices follows species counterpoint constraints (no parallel fifths/octaves, proper voice leading)
2. **Harmonic rhythm** — all voices share a common harmonic progression but express it differently
3. **Rhythmic independence** — each voice uses a different rhythmic pattern (polyrhythm across all voices)
4. **Motivic development** — themes introduced in one voice appear transformed in other voices (like Bach fugue subjects)
5. **Tension/resolution cycles** — synchronized across all voices to create coherent emotional arc
6. **Frequency optimization** — each voice stays within its critical band to prevent masking

This can be algorithmically generated. The counterpoint rules are fully formalized (Fux 1725, extended by modern computational musicology). A generator program can produce unlimited unique sessions.
