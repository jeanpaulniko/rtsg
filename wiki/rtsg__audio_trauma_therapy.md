## Step 3: Construct the Signal (Inverse Fourier Therapy)

The **inverse Fourier technique** for trauma therapy:

1. **Assess** the user's I-vector using the Intelligence Inspector
2. **Identify** damaged dimensions (near-zero activations)
3. **Generate** a personalized audio stream where:
   - Damaged dimensions receive **maximum melodic density** in their frequency/pattern bands
   - Healthy dimensions receive **moderate activation** (maintenance)
   - The polyphonic mix is **unique to each user's trauma profile**
4. **Deliver** as passive listening (headphones, speakers, sleep audio)
5. **Re-assess** periodically and update the audio profile

### Example: User with Spatial + Interpersonal Trauma

A user who was institutionalized (like Saint Cabrini's) might show:
- d_S (Spatial) = low (confined spaces, no environmental exploration)
- d_I (Interpersonal) = low (trust violation, institutional neglect)

Their personalized audio stream would:
- **Maximize binaural spatial movement** — melodies that pan, rotate, create 3D depth perception
- **Maximize call-and-response patterns** — voices that "converse," imitate each other, create the auditory experience of cooperative social interaction
- **Maintain other dimensions** at baseline activation levels

### Example: User with Kinesthetic + Interoceptive Trauma

A user with chronic pain or physical abuse history might show:
- d_K (Kinesthetic) = low (body avoidance, disconnection from physical sensation)
- d_C (Interoceptive) = low (unable to read internal states)

Their personalized audio stream would:
- **Maximize rhythmic entrainment** — strong, embodied beat patterns that entrain motor cortex
- **Maximize tension/resolution cycles** — harmonic progressions designed to activate the insula (interoceptive center)
- **Graduated intensity** — start gentle, increase complexity as the cortical regions reactivate
