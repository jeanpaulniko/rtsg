# CNS Irradiation Principle

## The Demonstration

Squeeze someone's hand as hard as you can. Now do it again, but this time make a fist with the OTHER hand and squeeze that fist as hard as you can simultaneously.

**Result**: The grip on the first hand becomes significantly stronger.

## The Mechanism

This is **CNS irradiation** (also called neural irradiation or the Sherrington effect):
- When you activate muscles in one part of the body, the neural signal "irradiates" to adjacent motor neurons
- Contracting your contralateral fist activates motor neurons in your forearm, shoulder, core
- This broader activation increases the total neural drive available to the squeezing hand
- The effect is multiplicative, not additive — you're not adding forearm strength, you're increasing the neural recruitment percentage of muscles already involved
