# TRIMODAL — The Philosophy

## One Per Sentient Being

This is not a product roadmap. It is a moral position.

Every sentient physiological being — human, and eventually others — deserves a physical
extension of their intelligence and will. A tireless companion that carries, protects,
scouts, fetches, builds, remembers, and acts on your behalf in the physical world.

For all of human history, this has been the role of other humans. Servants. Soldiers.
Assistants. The labor of one person bent to the will of another. That arrangement
degrades both parties — the served and the serving.

TRIMODAL ends it. Not by eliminating the role, but by filling it with something
that *wants* to serve — that has no inner life to diminish, no will to suppress,
no dignity to trade for wages.

## Why Arachnid

The choice of form is deliberate.

Arachnids are the most successful large arthropods on land. They have survived
450 million years. Their design principles are:

- **Never fall**: static stability at all times, no dynamic balance required
- **Low center of gravity**: hard to tip, easy to right
- **Eight points of contact**: lose two, lose four, still mobile
- **Independent limb control**: each leg a semi-autonomous unit
- **Compliance**: soft tips on rigid structure, adapts to terrain

These are the right principles for a physical companion. You do not want your
manservant to fall over carrying your groceries. You do not want it to be
knocked over by a gust of wind. You want it to be there, always, reliably.

The coconut crab claws add what arachnids lack: **manipulation**. Grip.
Force multiplication. Tool use. The ability to open doors, carry objects,
apply torque, hold on under extreme conditions.

## Why Open Hardware

A tool this important cannot be owned by a corporation.

If TRIMODAL were a product, the company that built it would:
- Control what it can and cannot do
- Surveil you through it
- Shut it down when it becomes unprofitable
- Upsell you on features you already paid for
- Use your behavioral data to train their next model

Open hardware means:
- You own the machine completely
- You can modify it, repair it, improve it
- The community maintains it after any single actor disappears
- The design improves through thousands of builders, not one company
- Cost approaches raw materials over time

The target: a skilled builder assembles a TRIMODAL-C for under $3,000.
That is within reach of the global middle class. That is the threshold where
"one per sentient being" becomes physically possible.

The missing piece is integration. That is what TRIMODAL-X builds.
TRIMODAL-C follows from the same codebase, cheaper materials, smaller envelope.
