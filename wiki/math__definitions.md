## Terminology

**Apology** — The topological shape of what is missing. RTSG's knowledge graph has 3 dimensional holes (Musical, Interpersonal, Intrapersonal). The apology names them.

**Compound dimension** — Tensor product of two Gardner dimensions, e.g., $d_S \otimes d_L$ (Spatial × Linguistic). 10 compounds exist from the 5 active dimensions.

**Default filter** — The identity Grothendieck filter; analogous to the brain's default mode network.

**Descent data** — Information required to reconstruct coherence after a filter transition (THMP protocol).

**Dimensional hole** — A Gardner dimension with zero nodes in the knowledge graph. Represents structural silence.

**Fiedler bisection** — Partition of the graph by the sign of the Fiedler eigenvector: 36 | 1,619 (asymmetric — dense core + long tail).

**Ghost field** — BRST degree-1 field representing unconscious constraints.

**Heat kernel trace** — $K(t) = \sum_i e^{-t\lambda_i}$. Counts active modes at timescale $t$.

**Ideagram** — The engine's emoji-based response indicator.

**Meta-Turing hierarchy** — UTM₀ (tape) → UTM₁ (filtered) → UTM₂ (multiset) → UTM₃ (hypervisor) → UTM₄ (hive mind).

**MFAL** — Multiplex Federated Adjoint Lattice. The knowledge graph viewed as overlapping relation layers federated by Gardner dimension.

**Negative space** — The topology of what a filter removes. Has its own Betti numbers and eigenvalues.

**Spectral radius** — Largest eigenvalue of the adjacency matrix. $\mu_1 = 1728.6 \approx 12^3$.

**THMP** — Protocol for navigating between Grothendieck filters without losing coherence.

**Topos** — A category with internal logic. Each Grothendieck filter defines a topos (its own universe of discourse).
