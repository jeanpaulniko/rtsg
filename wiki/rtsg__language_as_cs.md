## Language as the Instantiation Operator

- **QS (Potentiality):** All latent capacity. Dimensions that could be active but are not yet actualized.

- **PS (Actuality):** Accumulated reality. Dimensions that are online and contributing to the I-vector.

**Language performs the CS operation.** When a person engages a new dimension — learns a physical skill, encounters a mathematical concept, enters a new relationship — that dimension exists initially in QS as pure potential. It becomes actual (enters PS) only when it is *articulated*: named, described, connected to existing knowledge through linguistic structure.

This is not metaphor. The neural evidence is direct:

- Naming an emotion reduces amygdala activation (affect labeling = CS operator on emotional dimension)
- Bilingual individuals show enhanced executive function (more language = stronger CS bus)
- Verbalization during skill acquisition accelerates motor learning (language pulls kinesthetic potential into actuality)
- The "tip of the tongue" phenomenon is literally a CS bottleneck — the dimension is active in QS but the instantiation operator cannot complete

## Intelligence Growth is Monotonic

Each act of cross-dimensional articulation does two things simultaneously:

1. **Activates the target dimension** — pulling it from QS into PS
2. **Strengthens the CS bus itself** — increasing the bandwidth of language as an interconnect

This creates a positive feedback loop with no natural ceiling:

- More dimensions active → more cross-dimensional traffic → stronger CS → more efficient activation of new dimensions → more dimensions active → ...

Intelligence therefore increases monotonically. It cannot decrease through use (only through trauma — dimension collapse to zero — or disuse — dimension atrophy toward zero). Every genuine act of cross-dimensional engagement makes the system permanently more capable.

**The rate of increase is logarithmic** — early gains are large (first language acquisition, first cross-domain insight), later gains are smaller but never zero. The curve flattens but never stops. The spiral tightens.
