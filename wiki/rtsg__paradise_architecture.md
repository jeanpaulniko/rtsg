- Every transaction (exchange of value, commitment, agreement) is recorded on both sides
- Blockchain provides immutability — records cannot be retroactively altered
- Double ledger provides accountability — every credit has a corresponding debit
- This is the Digital Castle's verification mechanism: public actions are auditable, private space is inviolable

## Why Double Ledger, Not Single

Single ledger (standard blockchain): records THAT something happened.
Double ledger: records WHAT was exchanged on BOTH sides.

- Party A commits to deliver X → recorded on A's debit side and B's credit side
- Party B commits to deliver Y → recorded on B's debit side and A's credit side
- Fulfillment is verified → both entries are settled
- Breach is visible → the unsettled entry is public record

This eliminates the fundamental problem of trust in human interaction: you no longer need to trust the other party's word. The ledger is the word. The math is the trust.
