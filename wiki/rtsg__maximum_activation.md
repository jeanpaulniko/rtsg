## Key Insight

Each individual activity is ordinary and trivial. The insight is COMBINATORIAL — stacking activities to maximize the union of activated dimensions.

Cross-dimensional edges scale as k choose 2:
- 6 dims = 15 edges
- 8 dims = 28 edges
- 10 dims = 45 edges
- 12 dims = 66 edges — MAXIMUM

Going from 6 to 12 active dimensions quadruples the cross-edge count. The returns are superlinear.

## Design Principles

1. Redundancy is good: Multiple activities feeding the same dimension increases signal strength
2. Coverage is better: Adding one NEW dimension is worth more than doubling an existing one — marginal edge gain = current active dims, not 0
3. Sensory channels are independent: Vision, audition x2, olfaction, tactile, proprioceptive, and motor channels all run in parallel without bottleneck
4. Cognitive load distributes: Chess can be casual/intuitive while lecture is focal attention — different cognitive modes do not compete
5. The body is the platform: Most dimensions — Kinesthetic, Somatic, Interoceptive, Proprioceptive, Naturalistic — are body-based. You cannot reach 12/12 sitting in a chair staring at a screen.

## The Cheapest Upgrade

Sitting at desk coding = about 3 dimensions: Linguistic, Mathematical, Abstract.
Same coding + music + standing desk + bare feet + incense = about 7 dimensions.
Cost of upgrade: near zero. Edge gain: 3 to 21 — a 7x increase.

The cheapest cognitive upgrade is almost always adding a sensory/somatic channel, not optimizing an existing cognitive one.

Intelligence is purchasable with TIME and WILL. The currency is hours spent in high-activation states. The compound interest is the quadratic scaling of cross-edges.
