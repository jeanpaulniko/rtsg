## Core Thesis
Learning sign language is the single most powerful intelligence upgrade available to any human being, because it activates THREE dimensions simultaneously per word (Kinesthetic + Visual + Linguistic), and if you're hearing, FOUR (add Auditory). These are not additive — they are MULTIPLIERS.

## The Ogden-ASL Fusion

### C.K. Ogden's Basic English (1930)
- 850 carefully selected words cover 90% of concepts in a 25,000-word dictionary
- Only 18 verbs ('operators')
- Designed as: international medium + intro to full English + plain English
- Drew from Ogden & Richards' 'The Meaning of Meaning' (1923)
- George Bernard Shaw partly financed the development

## Research Backing

### Measured Effects of ASL on Intelligence
- **Pennsylvania University (Daniels, 10-year study)**: Children taught ASL alongside normal curriculum had higher reading levels and 15-20% vocabulary improvement
- **UC Davis / Cal State (20-year longitudinal)**: Babies taught sign language showed heightened reasoning skills and raised IQ by average of 12 points
- **University of Massachusetts**: ASL users process complex spatial information faster and make more intuitive observations
- **Northwestern University**: Even HEARING infants who observe ASL show improved cognition and object categorization
- **Gallaudet VL2 Lab**: ASL/English bilingualism strengthens brain networks for BOTH language and reading
- **UConn**: To young brains, language IS language whether signed or spoken — same neural architecture

### Framework Explanation of These Results
- Standard language: fires Auditory + Linguistic = 2 dimensions, 1 edge
- Sign language: fires Visual + Kinesthetic + Proprioceptive + Linguistic = 4 dimensions, 6 edges
- Bilingual (spoken + signed): fires ALL 5 = 5 dimensions, 10 edges
- The IQ gains, vocabulary gains, spatial processing gains are ALL predicted by the edge multiplication
- The framework doesn't just explain the research — it predicts it
